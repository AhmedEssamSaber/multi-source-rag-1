# docs/brain_edema.txt

Title: Brain Edema

Definition:
Brain edema is swelling caused by excess fluid within brain tissue.

Main Types:

* Vasogenic edema: disruption of blood-brain barrier, common with tumors
* Cytotoxic edema: cellular swelling, common with ischemia
* Interstitial edema: related to hydrocephalus

Common Causes:

* Brain tumors
* Stroke
* Trauma
* Infection
* Hemorrhage

Imaging Findings:

* T2/FLAIR hyperintensity
* White matter swelling
* Sulcal effacement
* Mass effect in severe cases

Symptoms:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Confusion
* Weakness
* Seizures
* Reduced consciousness

Treatment:

* Treat underlying cause
* Corticosteroids often used in tumor-related vasogenic edema
* Osmotic therapy in selected emergencies
* Surgical decompression in severe cases

Clinical Note:
Severe edema can become life-threatening due to raised intracranial pressure.
