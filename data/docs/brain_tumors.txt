Title: Brain Tumors

Definition:
Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells داخل المخ أو الأنسجة المحيطة به. They may arise primarily from the brain or spread from cancers elsewhere in the body.

Main Categories:

* Primary brain tumors: originate in the brain or meninges.
* Secondary (metastatic) tumors: spread from another cancer site.
* Benign tumors: slower growth, less invasive.
* Malignant tumors: faster growth, may infiltrate nearby tissue.

Common Examples:

* Meningioma
* Glioma
* Pituitary adenoma
* Metastasis
* Schwannoma

Common Symptoms:

* Headache
* Seizures
* Weakness or numbness
* Vision changes
* Balance problems
* Memory or personality changes
* Nausea or vomiting

Diagnosis:
MRI with contrast is commonly the best imaging study for characterization. CT may be used in emergency settings or to detect hemorrhage or calcification.

Important Imaging Findings:

* Mass lesion
* Contrast enhancement
* Edema
* Mass effect
* Midline shift
* Hydrocephalus

Treatment:
Depends on tumor type, grade, size, location, and patient condition.

* Observation
* Surgery
* Radiation therapy
* Chemotherapy
* Targeted therapy

Clinical Note:
Imaging suggests possibilities but final diagnosis may require pathology.
