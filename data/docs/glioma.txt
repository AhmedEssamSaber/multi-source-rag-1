# docs/glioma.txt

Title: Glioma

Definition:
Glioma is a tumor arising from glial cells in the brain. It includes astrocytoma, oligodendroglioma, and glioblastoma.

Behavior:
Gliomas range from lower-grade slower tumors to highly aggressive malignant tumors.

Common Symptoms:

* Headache
* Seizures
* Weakness
* Speech difficulty
* Cognitive decline
* Visual symptoms depending on location

MRI Findings:

* Intra-axial brain mass
* T2/FLAIR hyperintensity common
* Variable contrast enhancement
* Surrounding edema may be present
* Necrosis may be seen in high-grade tumors
* Infiltrative margins may occur

High-Grade Features:

* Ring or irregular enhancement
* Necrosis
* Rapid growth
* Significant edema
* Mass effect

Differential Diagnosis:

* Metastasis
* Abscess
* Lymphoma
* Demyelinating lesion

Treatment:

* Surgery when feasible
* Radiation therapy
* Chemotherapy
* Molecular-guided treatment in selected cases

Prognosis:
Depends on tumor grade, subtype, molecular profile, and extent of resection.
