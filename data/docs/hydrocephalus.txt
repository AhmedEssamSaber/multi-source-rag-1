# docs/hydrocephalus.txt

Title: Hydrocephalus

Definition:
Hydrocephalus is abnormal accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid causing enlargement of the ventricular system.

Types:

* Obstructive (non-communicating)
* Communicating
* Normal pressure hydrocephalus

Common Symptoms:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Gait disturbance
* Cognitive decline
* Urinary symptoms
* Decreased consciousness in severe cases

Imaging Findings:

* Enlarged ventricles
* Transependymal CSF flow may be seen
* Periventricular signal changes
* Possible obstructing mass or stenosis

Common Causes:

* Tumor
* Hemorrhage
* Infection
* Congenital obstruction
* Post-surgical changes

Treatment:

* CSF diversion such as shunt
* Endoscopic third ventriculostomy in selected patients
* Treat underlying cause

Clinical Note:
Acute hydrocephalus may be urgent.
