# docs/meningioma.txt

Title: Meningioma

Definition:
Meningioma is an extra-axial tumor arising from arachnoid cap cells of the meninges. It is one of the most common tumors involving the meninges.

Typical Behavior:
Many meningiomas are slow growing and benign, but some higher-grade forms are more aggressive.

Epidemiology:

* More common in women
* Usually diagnosed in adults
* May be associated with prior radiation or neurofibromatosis type 2

Common Symptoms:

* Headache
* Seizures
* Weakness
* Vision problems
* Personality or cognitive changes
* May be asymptomatic if small

MRI Findings:

* Extra-axial dural-based mass
* Broad dural attachment
* Strong homogeneous enhancement
* Dural tail may be present
* Adjacent vasogenic edema may occur
* Mass effect possible

CT Findings:

* Hyperdense lesion may be seen
* Calcification possible
* Hyperostosis may occur

Common Locations:

* Convexity
* Parasagittal region
* Sphenoid wing
* Olfactory groove
* Skull base

Differential Diagnosis:

* Dural metastasis
* Solitary fibrous tumor
* Schwannoma depending on location

Treatment:

* Observation in selected cases
* Surgical resection
* Radiation therapy in residual or recurrent disease

Prognosis:
Many grade 1 tumors have favorable outcomes. Higher grades carry greater recurrence risk.
