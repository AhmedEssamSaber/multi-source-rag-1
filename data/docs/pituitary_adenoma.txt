# docs/pituitary_adenoma.txt

Title: Pituitary Adenoma

Definition:
Pituitary adenoma is a tumor arising from the pituitary gland. Many are benign.

Types:

* Microadenoma: less than 10 mm
* Macroadenoma: 10 mm or larger
* Functioning tumors: secrete hormones
* Nonfunctioning tumors

Common Symptoms:

* Headache
* Visual field defects
* Hormonal imbalance
* Menstrual changes
* Galactorrhea
* Fatigue

MRI Findings:

* Sellar or suprasellar mass
* Enlargement of pituitary gland
* Contrast enhancement variable
* Optic chiasm compression in macroadenoma
* Cavernous sinus invasion may occur

Differential Diagnosis:

* Rathke cleft cyst
* Craniopharyngioma
* Meningioma in sellar region

Treatment:

* Medical therapy for some hormone-secreting tumors
* Surgery
* Radiation in selected cases

Prognosis:
Often favorable with proper endocrine and neurosurgical management.
